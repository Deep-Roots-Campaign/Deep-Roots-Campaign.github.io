Aerendyl 44

Day 4 of Firecrest, T.A. 515? | Day 13 in the Feywild
I had time on my hands before the first few of us would go to bed, so I finished my conversation with Toraash. Their insights on the Spellplague and the evolution of magic have given me much to ponder and research in the future, both at their request and for the sake of my own rekindled curiosity.

It also turned out that Oona, a fallen companion of Faen's and an old friend of Toraash’, is … here, sort of? I still don't fully understand. The shape of this shrine is equal to Oona's form when they were still around, but these are not their remains, I was told. Either way, I made sure to say goodbye on Toraash’ behalf before we left.

After that, I worked on a ring for myself; something to help replace the forefinger I lost in Baheera, albeit with a little extra functionality. Deidre's help was incredibly useful. The job isn't done yet, but we're well on our way. I've also made sure we have plenty of powdered silver and iron, and while rummaging through my pack, I also discovered I had more holy water that I just forgot about. It's all about the little things, right?

I also asked Mielikki for some water from her pond. I imagined I'd need it to clean myself off after what I had planned for Grout (read on if you wish to learn, whoever you might be …). That, and refilling my waterskin, of course. For all the caution we've had to employ in terms of giving back when we take something, a small seed from the dreamgrass flower she appreciated last time only seemed fitting. [ Imagine a messy sketch of a weed leaf here ]

When it was time to take first watch, I settled in with Luck. I think this is only the second time since he joined our little band of misfits that I've had a moment alone with him, actually. I'm happy I got around to having a conversation with him in private, as it led to me trusting him with the acorn of resurrection. Of course I would have rather held on to it myself, but I know that is only the difficulty of relinquishing control speaking. At least it's not greed; I'll take comfort in that.

I simply figured with his focus homing in on his allegiance to the Raven Queen and her tenets, he will be the least likely to abuse it – whether intentionally or not –, along with myself. Although our reasons for it may be different, with myself seeking to maintain the delicate balance between life and death, and him … not necessarily, I suppose, the point stands. Besides, who knows where I might wind up once we encounter Vestan. I wouldn't put it past myself to end up somewhere well out of reach of someone who needs what the acorn can provide if or when the time for that comes.

My attempt to reach Da'yin once I got to sleep was frustratingly brief, but her assurance of my mother's safety in the Tower of Ionna brought some relief. Her plea for my return weighs heavily on my heart, though, especially given the dangers we face — and the voracious hole left in place of what could've only been the urge to cry at receiving this news about her. It's so much easier to cope with when it simply doesn't cross your mind …

A few hours later, the began with unexpected gifts and preparations. Corvaux gifted me a lump of adamantine and some bloodseeker arrows — a welcome surprise! I wonder if them finding it here (at least, I presume they found it here) has anything to do with Oona perhaps having touched down here during their fall? It is, after all, an ore found in meteor craters.

But there was more to this morning, of course: I bound Grout to me as my familiar! Granted, it almost went sideways and I think it would've taken only one more loss of focus for us both to tumble into one of those pools around us, but it panned out in the end. Little man’s got a beautiful pair of wings on him now, and eyes so luminescent, I fully expect them to frighten me in the dark on at least one occasion.

Before we set out, Deidre, Mirri and Merisande went back up to try one last time to get rid of the bloodied handprints, but that – apparently – didn't go according to plan. I'm not entirely sure what happened, but, in any case, we'll need to address the matter by ‘addressing’ Vestan, I suppose.

We then finally ventured deeper into the Underfey, where we all too sound came upon a fork in the road. Both paths revealed the presence of Vestan's cultists nearby. Our group has split to handle the threat — Corvaux, Luck, Merisande and I heading right to confront the lone orc, while Mirri, Smoke and Deidre attempt to gather information from the group on the left.
