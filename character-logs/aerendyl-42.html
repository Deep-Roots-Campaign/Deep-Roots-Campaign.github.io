Day 3 of Firecrest, T.A. 515? | Day 12 in the Feywild
The aftermath of our battle with the Will-o'-Wisps left us battered, but victorious. We briefly rested to recover and regroup, setting the stage for further exploration of these subterranean chambers. With that and Alixandria's parting words, I suppose we're already on the Underfey's doorstep.

Luck and I made our way up, and I'm glad to learn I have not lost my magic touch entirely. Had the contrary been true, Luck might have wound up flattened by a misplaced boulder, rather than able to breeze right past it. Above ground, our discovery of Grout and Ralph playing hide-and-seek brought a moment of lighthearted relief amidst the tension. Their carefree interaction was a poignant reminder of the simple joys I nowadays often overlook …

Upon our return below ground ( with our furry companions, Luck included ), we wound up in a chamber that appears to be a shrine to the Serenity of the Sisters — precisely whom Wisp had advised us to seek!

The altars, or rather pools, dedicated to various goddesses and powerful entities like Faen, Mielikki, the Librarian, Carroway, Amara, and Dahma, offer a wealth of potential aid and knowledge. The inclusion of the Librarian, a rebel archfey whose primary concern – as far as I know – is to spread knowledge, among their number is particularly intriguing and potentially useful in our struggle, or various struggles.

The mural on the ceiling, depicting "the beast who devours the sun," meanwhile revealed crucial information about the nature of eclipses in the Feywild. The sun here isn't merely a source of light, but a portal to other realms, shifting dangerously during an eclipse. In its usual state, it should lead to the Fire Plane. Fitting enough, but the revelation about the sun being a rift between worlds didn't stop there: it leads into the Abyss as it shifts towards a total eclipse, before opening a way into the Shadowfell for the briefest of moments once total darkness is attained, only for the pattern to then be reversed again.

As nightmarish a finding as this is given the circumstances, this could very well be the key to understanding Vestan's ultimate goal. The warning – or challenge – about the risks and potential rewards of being out during an eclipse cements the conviction that he seeks to manipulate the next upcoming eclipse. Now that spiel about ‘blotting out the sun’ makes even more sense …

Either way, I now await word from Wisp. I hope she knows when the next eclipse here will occur. As a druid of the Circle of Stars, she of all people might have an idea about this, I suppose.

Deidre meanwhile put up quite the show, enticing lustermites to dance to her tune and uncovering a slew of additional names belonging to the Serenity of the Sisters – before she decides to drink from Mielikki's pool. Now, I am not wont to mistrust deities per se, but this to me seemed an unnecessarily brazen move. However, the Feywild and the powers that be there have a way of proving me wrong at every turn: the mouthful of water yielded a mysterious diamond.

All in all: a strange blend of dire discovery and light-hearted communication, at least until Mirri's communion with Hallow went awry. A gut-wrenching scream heralded her return to the here and now before storming out of the shrine.

( Was Hallow perhaps no longer there, or wounded, or no longer herself, even? Did the locathi get to either of them? )
I had to chase after her. How could I not, even against the odds that she might not even want to see me or anyone else as she confronted … whatever pains that happening stirred to life?

Her anguish, the blood, the scrubbing and the cryptic mantra about being "the harbinger of her own fate" hint at a burden far heavier than I had imagined.

She kept staring at her reflection in the water. Her refusal to truly tell me who she expected to see there – or perhaps was afraid to see – was expected, but hard to swallow all the same. She referenced the scar on her cheek from a previous encounter in which she chose honesty regarding what she deemed plain to see as a reason not to do so. It hurts to think she might believe her present company could do such a thing to her, but then … how can I blame or even judge her? We don't all deal with pain by sharing it.

She also referred the promise she made to get me home — a risky thing I believe, now we know what we know about promise magic. This, I don't fully understand. How does that impact her ability to be truthful? The depth of her trauma and the secrets she carries are obviously taking a toll, and I worry about the impact this will have on our her, our objective and our group as a whole.

Ah … shit, I don't know. If there's anything I've learned, it's that I know very little, and can infer even less.

For now, we have more work to do. The information about the eclipse and its potential role in Vestan's plans adds to our urgency, so we must swiftly decipher these clues, harness any power and wisdom offered by the Serenity of the Sisters, and find a way to stop Vestan before he can manipulate these cosmic forces for his sinister purposes.
