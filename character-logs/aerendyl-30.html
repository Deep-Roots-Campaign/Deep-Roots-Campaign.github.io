Aerendyl 30

Day 30 of Bloomweave, 515 T.A.?
Oh, the dating is entirely off now, I suppose. I now have reason to believe the days pass about four times faster back on Rakāuvi and back at home. Or maybe it's three – I need to gather my thoughts about it whenever next I have a spare moment, I believe. In any case ...

Last night, as I drifted into the realm between waking and dreaming, I found myself once more in the presence of Da'yin. Her visage was not as I remembered – its geodesic properties having grown dull, grey almost, and her surroundings bare-bones in comparison to the chambers lined with shelves and books, jars, ingredients … Her home, apparently, lies now in ruins, and three of her siblings were lost to the cruelty of corrupt Roshmarian mages. These vile cultists (always the cultists of late …), disciples of the Lord of Lies himself, have a history of enslaving primordials. Da'yin warned me against venturing near Umbrosia and the infamous Rashmore Tower.

If nothing else, this served as a reminder of the centuries of suffering endured by the dao at the hands of mages, demons, and other malevolent entities. To think I once wondered why they're so secretive, isolated … When I offered my aid, Da'yin's response was measured — unless I possessed knowledge of the Roshmarian mages or a pure source of aether ore, my assistance from afar would be limited.

I won't deny still feeling forlorn about the knowledge that I cannot abandon my current quest; not unless expressly bidden to do so, as determined by our agreement. Vestan's threat looms too large, threatening the very fabric of existence. With a heavy heart, I conveyed my predicament to Da'yin, who, in her wisdom, did not begrudge me. Instead, she assured me she has handled worse, that she will rebuild — and even imparted a spell that would allow me to detect sources of pure elemental power, a tool to aid in locating imprisoned genies and, perhaps one day, guide me home through elemental vortices.

Before our communion drew to a close, I shared the events that had transpired since our last encounter. I spoke of Thaddeus' fateful deal, the coming and going of companions, and the looming shadow of Vestan Tlanbourn. But it was the star I'm pursuing that captivated Da'yin — a beacon of pure Primordial energy, a cosmic lynchpin. She spoke of legends, of the "Forge of Apotheosis” some of these stars were captured to fuel … This legend is unknown to me, but something tells me I'll learn the truth of it soon enough.

Naturally, I couldn't leave without mentioning the hag's eye, described by some Fey, apparently, as a ‘fell curse’. Da'yin warned me said curse would only grow in power with time, and advised encasing it in lead for now, or even feeding it to some insatiable creature for now, cautioning against confronting the hag directly. I'm not entirely sure I'll find a creature voracious enough to solve this problem for me, so I must look to alternatives: to convince the hag she holds no sway over me (fat chance, says I), or redirect her gaze elsewhere … A plan hatched then and there: what if I can redirect the hag's malice towards Vestan himself?

Of course, the conversation inevitably turned to my parents, to the revelation of my father's entanglement with Vestan's machinations. Fear gripped me—what if Vestan's cultists sought vengeance against him, now that he'd been free? Was he even free, still? Was my mother? Da'yin, in her compassion, offered to have another see if my mother's safe and deliver a message on my behalf; a warning, more like.

Once I awoke, the world seemed to shift. Mirri, her hands wreathed in inky darkness, climbed through the window, a gelatinous familiar from the Abyss in tow. There was hardly any time to ponder this development—a shadow, cold and strange, lunged at Mirri amidst her ritual to identify a demon-possessed bottle.

The battle was brief but intense, the shadow speaking in Abyssal upon its defeat. Mirri, visibly shaken, revealed it was an old friend, promising to explain when her composure returned. The ritual resumed, unveiling the bottle as road to Orcus' domain, the 333rd layer of the Abyss. So, not the 108th as I suspected, but I suppose that would have been too great a coincidence, wouldn't it?

As we discussed how to dispose of this vile artefact, a few ideas took root. The hatred between devils and demons was legendary—perhaps a devil could destroy what we could not. Or, I reasoned as one of several not particularly drawn to the idea of bargaining with a devil (I'll not follow Thaddeus’ example there), I could seek out another portal to the Abyss and cast the bottle into its depths.

The hag's eye remained a pressing concern, too. We explored options—encasing it in lead or feeding it to a Xorn or other ravenous creature to buy time. I revealed my interest in slipping it to Vestan himself as a way to trick the hag into surrendering her hold over me.

Mirri's revelation about the hag's red hair – per Deidre's description, assuming this even is the same hag she's acquainted with! – sparked determination: we would inflict this curse upon Vestan if given the chance. Apparently, redheads are his type …

Luck, too, faces a curse of his own — why would he keep clear of curses after just having escaped one the pixies subjected him to? This time, the curse is centred around an axe he took from the chamber with artefacts under Deidre's mansion, jealous and needy, identified by Mirri's magic. He and I at least share a goal: to be rid of the items that seek to make our lives miserable!

Gods, we have much to take care of in the coming days …
