Luck of the Draw 28

Feywild journal
Property of Luck
5th entry since being able to leave Krumholtz.

The gaps in these entries are not going to improve, apologies to future me when they get really confused trying to remember what happened between meeting Deidre and leaving that manor with a myconid we met in 100ft deep hole. Though I really should get better at writing down my experiences in the Feywild.

In short and not necessarily in order, we explored the manor briefly and I personally got to enjoy sunlight for the first time in I don't know how long. The manor Deidre was bound to seems to have belonged to Eristalon, but where we needed to go to free Deidre was actually below the manor. There were several revelations related to Aeren and a journal they've been carrying around which had us certain this journal cannot ever end up in the wrong hands, not to mention an eventual reveal that a hags eye was watching Aeren. We learned Deidre doesn't have a pulse, explored the strange place under the manor which was full of various treasures, strange items, and unfortunately many many dead to the point that more than one room had the very walls packed with bones.

I'm still not quite sure how exactly we freed Deidre, when we found the room with a strange ritual circle surrounding a place someone would be chained to the floor, Mirri had us leave the room. I was wary, but there wasn't much I could do alone to press for answers. But after some time, we all met up again and Deidre said they could leave this place. Before that however Deidre wanted to lay to rest her friend Kai, which led to us entering the aforementioned 100ft hole. I feel like I should've done more in that place, I worship a death goddess and contributed little more than vague memories of elven funeral rites. I know in my heart that I can contribute more, I cannot disappoint them.

Im not sure how to explain the 'star' Aeren was able to hear while we were down there. To him it was deafening but it took some effort for me to hear and then make out words from the noise. We were going to take a boat and find the source of this noise but the boat belonged to a myconid a short distance from the vessel. They introduced themselves as Chip, and mistook me for a member of the 'Rusted Swords' I decided to go with the lie, maybe gain just that much more favor at least with Chip. I did have to explain myself to Deidre, who believed my lie and wondered if I knew her brother. I admittedly felt a bit guilty for the lie after that.

Since Chip wanted to take us to a settlement called the Blight Commune, I returned to the surface along with Aeren and Deidre. Aeren and myself collected our animal companions while Deidre packed some of her things that remained in the manor. The three of us took a surface route that would be easier on Ralph before rejoining the others.

Before we continued following Chip to the Blight Commune however, Mirri had many words for us regarding Vestan Tlanbourn and our reckless behavior. Words that I don't care to repeat here, but ones that resonated with me when they were said. I think I've slipped too far into old habits, getting cocky and reckless and fantasizing again about all the fun lies and scams I could pull. As if I learned nothing at all being sent to Winter, or getting myself stranded in the Krumholtz Mountains. I even went and managed to get myself cursed, it seems like an insignificant one right now but I don't think I could admit it to the party after everything that got said. Especially not after seeing that look Mirri had when Aeren revealed Malachai was the 'Stonecutter' aka Aerens father.

I must better myself, act out the will of the Raven Queen, be of genuine aid to my new allies, and survive to finally leave the Feywild. If I cannot improve, then I just might get myself or others killed.

P.S. With my mind able to wonder about the material plane, I started dwelling on what the years following my 'disappearance' might've been like. Did anyone miss me? Is my old home still standing? Did I get a funeral?
