Mirri 37

Flamecrest, 515 of 3A.
Day 12 in the Feywilds

I have now met my Patron’s jailor, the lochathi, as Hallow called it. It was as pleasant an introduction as could be expected, all things considered. The bruising will soon fade. And Hallow’s concern, her encompassing love, however strangely expressed… What would I do without her? Where would I turn? What would I have become?

I know the answer to that already.

We did not speak of Mama… but around her, I suppose. Around what she had done. Either way, I knew I was understood. It is maddening, sometimes, that there are perhaps only two beings left in all the universe who can understand me. One of them is Hallow. The other…

Well. I suppose I will never really know if that’s true, whatever that blackened part of my heart tells me. There will only ever be the one chance to find out.

Aerendyl seemed half-crazed this morning, same as me, eager to explain about a strange visitor he had in the night, in dreams. I was content to keep my own tale to myself, only asking after the lochathi. I made it as quick as possible. I asked my question and they turned to me in alarm, fearing that I’d been harmed. I—

I hated it. I cannot stand their eyes on me, their concern, their… their fear for me. I don’t want it. I don’t want them to look at me like that, to put blankets over my shoulders like Corvaux did in the Blight Commune, to reach for my hand, any of it. I don’t want it because I know in a matter of days it will all be snatched away.

Best case scenario is that they ask me to leave and never travel with them again. Worst case…

The cut was deep. It took months to heal. And my friends are far more capable fighters than... I’d be lucky to get away with a scar this time.

Eventually Aeren described his own strange encounter. The star that we heard, that we came all this way for, is indeed inside the staff and… alive. Or something akin to that. Toraash, he said their name was, and they had spoken to him, curious about their new wielder and his strange companions. It is… heartbreaking and comforting both to see Aerendyl finally learn some suspicion, to be less trusting of everyone and everything in his path.

It’s also fascinating to know someone who just… stumbles into deals with entities of unfathomable power. Perhaps it was not Thaddeus I should have been afraid of bringing to him. Aerendyl could probably fucking have afternoon tea with Zirel.

And yet, through all this talk, I could not stop thinking of Hallow, of her offer to eviscerate my companions should they turn on me as I fear they will. It made me laugh, finally cheering me from the melancholy I’d slipped into, but… but it also twists my stomach with guilt.

Alixandria guided us to the main road, where wagons have been seen coming and going. Why wagons? Why any of this pomp at all? To commune with a being as Aerendyl and I did just last night should take hours, maybe days if the conversation is one that must stretch so long. I can understand an entourage of cultists around him, protecting and defending against, well, someone like me. It shouldn’t take weeks of time. What is he doing here? And why?

There was a broken wagon in our path. I hid my tattoos before I approached, knowing that this was either a trap or one of the many carts that belong to him.

The proprietor, Agate, seemed desperate to leave and Deidre, appearing out of thin air, started discussing her newfound faith in Dounuma. A welcome distraction. But something has shifted—Agate is speaking frantically about a “new god.” The oppressiveness of the sun. A blessed dark to blot out the light. The birth of a new god.

No. No, this isn’t what this is. It can’t be. It can’t.

I used the Shiftweave, wearing the very robes that he does. The instant recognition and reverence made me sick to my stomach. I played along, I called myself his shadow. I sent that goblin as far from this place as I could, warning of the lingering threat to his plans. So little of it was a lie.

The birth of a god. Blotting out the Sun. The Crown of the Faceless King. The books you stole from Hastaerien’s library. No. Please, gods, not this. You did not kill my baby brother for this. You did not barter my soul away for this.

Vestan… what have you done?
