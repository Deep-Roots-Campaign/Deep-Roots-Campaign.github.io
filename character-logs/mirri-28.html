Mirri 28

Bloomweave, 515 of 3A.
Near-end of Day 9 in the Feywilds

We’ve descended into the bowels of this place. Some of us preferred to jump headlong into the depths while the rest of us with more common sense climbed down carefully.
And thus began the grim task of finding Kai’s remains. Funerals and their rites are not something I am overly familiar with. Mama and I were hardly invited to the funerals of extended relatives, Rodan’s family is all still living, and… and I would rather not think of the rest.
It’s only part of why I’m so godsdamned angry with them right now. I don’t know the rites. I cannot lay them to rest if something were to happen to them.
After the bones were collected, Aerendyl described a “star song” that he could hear. He said it was near deafening, and after listening closely, I could hear it too. I couldn’t understand all of it, but I heard draconic words like “celebration” and “awaken.” I reached out to Hallow, wondering if this strange song was familiar in any way. Her confusion was palpable, but she was proud of me for finding it.

Yes, I love you too. I suppose we’re on our own, then. I need something fun after today. More than that, I need to talk with my companions, all of them. I have tried to warn them, but they don’t get it. He does not understand the concept of mercy and I do not want to watch them learn that as I did.
We stumbled across a small myconid named Chip when looking for a boat to borrow. Chip lives in a nearby village, the Blight Commune. What an unfortunate name. Luck quickly claimed membership with the Rusted Swords in an attempt to make us all look a bit better, but Deidre said that her brother, Mathias, was a member as well.
We’ve split up for now, with Aerendyl, Deidre, and Luck heading through the surface path so that they can collect their furry friends and Deidre can gather supplies to travel. Corvaux and I will stay below and take the boat with Chip.
Aerendyl’s problem with a Hag will certainly come back to bite us. Chip mentioned that the Stony Wood was where we might find such a creature to settle whatever debt our genasi friend has accrued.
I cannot stay silent any longer. They need to hear this. They have to know what he will do to them. What he has done to me.

The elf I found at the tea farm, Malachai, is the Stonecutter. The journal is his.

He’s Aeren’s father.

The eyes. Their eyes are the same. And Aeren is the same age that I was and he has the book he found and it’s happening again. It’s already begun. I can’t stop it, I can’t help him, I’ve failed again and

They cannot know. I can never tell them now.

I’m sorry. I’m so sorry. im sorry im sorry im sorry The words become an unintelligible mass of ink, nearly scratching through the paper.
