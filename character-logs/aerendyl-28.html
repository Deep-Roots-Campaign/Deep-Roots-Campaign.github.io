Aerendyl 28

Day 29 of Bloomweave, 515 T.A.?
The Well's depths greeted us with warmth and the gentle rush of distant waters, a stark contrast to the bones carpeting its bed. A small canoe bobbed beyond the entangled roots, while sated redcap mushrooms dotted the surroundings, their thirst for blood temporarily quenched by Corvaux's apparent intervention before we even got down here.

Deidre and I illuminated our path with a torch, and soon, she uncovered Kai's skeletal remains. As we all mused over Elven funerary rites, Deidre and Corvaux delicately retrieved the bones from the watery graves. With the skull in hand, Kai faded from view, exhaustion claiming his ethereal form.

Amidst the depths, the star's melody swelled, a symphony of wind and draconic phrases intertwined with an unfamiliar tongue. With some concentration, the others could hear it, too, to varying extents. Its theme was centred around “creation”, “celebration”, “awakening” … Somehow, perhaps because of this slow recognition, the sound became less eerie, and more so alien.

As we ventured towards the boat, a peculiar figure emerged from the shadows—a myconid, its mushroom cap crowning layered robes. Chip, this new acquaintance of ours from theBlight Commune, extended an invitation to their domain, but first, some of us – mainly Luck and I, with Deidre choosing to accompany us – needed to return to the surface to gather Grout, Ralph and possessions.

During our ascent – unfortunately at the cost of Corvaux plunging into the water once the Flight spell was cast on me … –, I offered to carry Luck and Deidre. Luck was easy enough to carry, and Deidre helped carry her weight by … well, clinging to me like a monkey of sorts. All very cute, and I admit I quite appreciated the embrace, though that appreciation turned to me being flustered as her fingers found my hair. It was a gentle, rather unfamiliar touch that set my cheeks ablaze. Her whispered praise only deepened the hue, and I found myself grateful for the fact she was holding onto my back and not my front, obscuring my flustered reaction.

‘Listen’ carefully, whoever might read this (shame on you for snooping!): it is not like I don't know how to accept affections, or even solicit them. She simply caught me unprepared. Anyway …

Reunited with Grout, Ralph, and our belongings, we descended once more below ground, the star's song resuming its ethereal refrain. But before we could decipher its meaning, Mirri called us to attention, her demeanour tense.

She spoke of Vestan Tlanbourn, the Elven sorcerer she'd hunted across planes for over half a century. His vile ambitions to tear asunder the fabric of reality and beckon forth the Abyss had left a trail of destruction, the recent carnage on a Sylvari farm a grim testament to his cruelty. Only young Nettie had survived, shielded by a nearby shrine's grace.

Well, Nettie, and Malachai—or the Scribe, as the cultists apparently called him, and the Stonecutter in most of my entries so far. It was apparently he who had painted the walls with bloody scripture, opening the rift into what must have been the 108th layer of hell—or an attempt at it. I don't actually know if that rift led to its intended destination, and at that point, I didn't bother to ask.

This moment felt pivotal, for we were ( and are ) close to the Fallen Halls where we hope to find Vestan. Now that the explicit ties between Vestan and Malachai were made clear, or the purpose the latter served to the former, I felt it was no longer appropriate for me to hold back the truth—especially not as the others seemed to grow progressively more suspicious of the trouble I had hiding my reactions to every mention of Malachai's name.

As the truth regarding who he was – or is – to me spilled forth ( my father ), Mirri's face drained of color. Fear, and something more inscrutable, contorted her features. She reveals she fears leading us to our doom—and perhaps me in particular, a genasi who always strived to attain Malachai's level of skill with stone and crystal. With Malachai having escaped Vestan with Mirri's and Runa's help, what if I’m considered the next best option?

Or worse: what if my mother is? Malachai did say he had no time to spare; that he had to protect his family. Unless he has other children I am unaware of … I must return home, and fast—the second this mess here has been resolved. I won't trust his word for it if he tells my mother is safe—or even if he tells me the opposite. I must see for myself … assuming not too much time has passed there, in comparison to the Feywild.

Now all the cards are on the table, at least as far as I can tell, we resolved to spend the last hours of the day seeking out this start – we're so close; I can feel it! – and gather our strength before confronting Tlanbourn. But as the hag's eye lingered in my pack, a reminder of unseen threats, I now can't help but wonder what twisted paths lie ahead, and what price we'll need to pay to see this journey through.
