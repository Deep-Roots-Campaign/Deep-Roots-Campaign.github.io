I want to preface this with making it known that I have never, and will never, regret following Aerendyl that day, nor any day afterwards. That includes when we went down into the ruins in search of his star. Knowing Aerendyl was able to get one of the things he wants more than makes up for anything else, and I hope that I'm able to help him even more in the future.

The Doordn egg I was able to retrieve also makes up for it. That'll either be a nice pet or a good lump of gold for us to put towards protection for Grout and Ralph against the Pale Blight.

Preface ended.

I had warned the party of the unusual vines on the roof earlier that morning. Now, as we made to leave with the star, Luck saw those same vines creeping down from our escape point. With trust that we would follow each other, we agreed to run past and escape. Mirri and I were the last ones to leave, but we eventually made it out. Unfortunately, we left only to find that the vines belonged to some kind of dragon. Did this explain Log's disappearance? Was Log now working for this dragon?

Regardless of any questions, I'm thankful that Deidre was able to keep Aeren and his star safe with invisibility from down in the tunnel. Upon seeing the state of things, and after hearing sounds of an area of the building collapsing on the opposite side to where Mirri and I was, our beautiful charlatan decided to cast some kind of illusion on herself before stepping out into the open. I have my suspicions on who it is, but gods damn are they gorgeous.

I fear that Mirri and I will have to have a very uncomfortable chat about that. If that is who I fear it is, I may very well fall victim to his charms. He's my type. Tall, beautiful hair, dazzling eyes, and he wears my favourite colour... Mirri, please tell me that isn't who I think it is.

... That aside, we have more pressing matters. (1/2)

(2/2) Deidre got severely hurt. I think the building collapsing was her attempt to deal with the dragon, but she seemed to have ended up in a worse state because of it. I was able to get onto the roof and call it Eristalon's Bitch to try and get it's attention, because hopefully grabbing it's attention means that Deidre will be able to flee to safety.

I have plans. Half-baked plans that rely on luck (not our fluffy, reliable charlatan, though I sincerely hope he is safe) and a whole lot of audacity. If I don't survive this, I am okay with that. As long as everyone else is able to escape and regroup, my death will be worth it.

And if I survive, I can brag about it.

Win/Win.
