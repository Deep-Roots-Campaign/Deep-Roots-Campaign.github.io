Aerendyl 33

Day 1 of Flamecrest, year 515 of T.A.?
During the stillness of night watch, Mirri indulged me in a chance to practice my hand at cartomancy. The Ettin card, a two-headed giant, spoke of her stubborn nature and inner turmoil. When asked if she would attain her heart's desire in the coming days, the answer admittedly eluded me, drowned out by the incessant song of the star I pursued. Instead, I inquired about her wishes—to unravel Vestan's mind, to understand the depths of his cruelty. Our bond deepened as I apologized for overstepping and offered my aid in her future scams, a partnership forged in shared purpose.

As dawn broke, Corvaux uncovered the truth about the ivy blanketing the roof—living creatures held at bay by the enigmatic fog. We resolved to leave them undisturbed, hopeful for safe passage when our time in the ruins drew to a close.

With Rorschach scouting ahead and Deidre's Shatter spell opening the way, we ventured into a hallway draped in darkness, its walls alive with moss and lichen. At its end, a door bore an unsettling inscription in Sylvan, speaking of corrupted fruit and poisonous ichor. The cold iron key transformed the passageway, revealing the path forward.

Descending into the depths, the star's song grew clearer, its melody haunting and insistent. Two doors barred our way, each demanding a tribute. Beyond them, chambers of petrified oak and earthen pots cradling Doordnn eggs. Corvaux's move to claim the intact egg unleashed the wrath of meenlocks, arachnid horrors bent on destroying innocence. Though victorious, I did not get away entirely unharmed ( nor my pride entirely unscathed ).

But it was the final chamber that held me captive—in a sense. There, enshrined in glass, rested a staff pulsing with the star's essence. An inexplicable connection drew me in, a note of longing, and as I reached out, the room came alive with shifting hues and resonant energies. Teal light radiated from my chest, a mirror of the staff's brilliance.

In a breath, I was somewhere else entirely, bound and helpless before an ancient, malevolent presence. Though, perhaps the word ‘before’ is the wrong one, for the presence was everywhere around me, in me, like an all-seeing eye, reading me like a book. Words poured forth unbidden, populating my mind with a verse steeped in foreboding shadow. It demanded a price, a piece of my very being, my core, my soul. Lost in its thrall, I relinquished the most fundamental memories shared between my mother and I, and with it, my longing for home. Though my mother's face still flickers in my mind, her light has dimmed, a fire turned to weak embers.

I have never experienced something as profoundly upsetting as knowing I once looked to something and someone for comfort, motivation and encouragement, only to now recall purely the factual about the memories that served that purpose; to be left unfeeling about the events that once tethered me to the Earth Plane. I feel unanchored, free-floating, and yet so incredibly heavy for it.

Once returned to the chamber, shaken and violated, I recited a cryptic verse as if possessed. The words I cannot remember, and perhaps it is for the better. The glass shield promptly fell away, and riddled with fear, I claimed the staff. Its power surged through me, banishing the lingering dread and melding with my own essence.

The star's song faded, its purpose fulfilled, leaving me forever changed. A piece of myself traded for this cosmic companion in a sense, a sacrifice I pray was not in vain.

Before all else, we had best leave these cursed halls. Not only did they once belong to Eristalon, but they still do, for if that voice, that entity, was not Eristalon himself, I'll eat both my boots. Whatever else the other chambers in this vault hold, it cannot be worth exposing ourselves to that foul presence; it cannot be worth others facing the choice between death – or somehow even worse – and sacrificing a piece of one's soul.
