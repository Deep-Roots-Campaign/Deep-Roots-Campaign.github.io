Aerendyl 37

Day 2 of Flamecrest, year 515 T.A.?

As we settled for breakfast, the mood at times bordered on carefree with Corvaux and Deidre jesting about blood soup. However, that levity soon gave way to more serious matters when Mirri brought up the locathi — a twisted, angelic creature that not only guards the 73rd layer of the Abyss, but it strips memories from its victims, taking from them what makes them … well, them. The description reminded me all too much of what I faced in the vault, alone and locked away as I was. The revelation that it had attempted to rip out Mirri's soul through her nostrils last night sent a chill down my spine.

It would be far too outlandish to think that somehow Eristalon, Old Oak, Lord Aurelius – whatever we might want to call him now – somehow involved one such locathi in extracting my memories from me, right? Right?

I suppose it doesn't matter at the moment. I should try reaching out to Da'yin again to see if she might have any ideas, and also to simply let her know what transpired since last we spoke some days ago.

Meanwhile, Deidre continues to prove evasive to our questions. When questioned about the conviction that she's being followed, I thought I'd sensed a lie in her voice — a dissonance between her composed face and her breaking voice that leaves me uneasy up until this very moment.

Luck's detection of necromantic traces – during an attempt to actually spot any unbidden onlookers – in the oak roots led to a fascinating, if somewhat disturbing, history lesson from Alixandria and Deidre about the forest's past. The idea of people turning into trees upon death is … strangely comforting. Though, given what this place has thrown at us so far, I wonder just how at peace the souls contained in those trees truly are.

Our discussion turned to the nature of the entity I've been communicating with. The fact that it speaks Celestial and Draconic suggests it isn't a demonic entity of any sort, which does allay our concern some. I shared my changed fate lines with the group, prompting Mirri to reveal her own tattoos — a physical manifestation of her pact with Hallow. The conversation about fate, especially Luck's warning about defying the Raven Queen, left me pondering our own destinies.

After informing the group Toraash would likely be listening in and gaining the group's consent to be transparent with Toraash when we commune, Mirri shared a detailed description of the ritual that bound her to Hallow. Her sketch in the sand of the intricate magical circle was both fascinating and sobering, knowing we might face something similar when confronting Vestan.

The possibility of entering the Fallen Halls in a mere couple of days through the Underfey presented new opportunities, challenges and fears. The descriptions of the dangers of travelling above ground – living statues, some blazing guardian, poisonous plants and who knows what else – left me apprehensive, and the description of what we might find in the Underfey didn't make me feel much better. We won't have a helpful native to guide us to a cosy little town this time we enter the Underfey, I fear. Yet, Mirri and Deidre seemed to prefer this route.

The revelation that nothing truly dies in the Fallen Halls added another layer of complexity to our mission. If Mirri kills Vestan there, will he truly be dead for good, or must we drag him out of the building so she can ... finish the job elsewhere?

What might happen if someone is meant to die there, during this ritual of Vestan's? Will they die? Will he have discovered some sort of loophole to exploit? I assume so, and that hardly puts me at ease. The knowledge that he had my father in his clutches until recently and his lackeys may well have recaptured him since, instills fear of the worst in me: my father might be there, incapacitated, waiting to be sacrificed in some horrific manner.

Truly, none of this – the retrospection, the fearing for the future – puts me at ease. There is precious little now, if anything, in fact, that I can think of to put my worries to rest. Even thoughts and memories of what once brought me joy now makes me wistful at best, and paranoid at worst.

As we broke camp and moved on, our attempts at stealth were mixed. The sight of enormous elk herded by a giant left me in awe. It was a welcome distraction of the mire in my head, as was our encounter with a goblin merchant, Agate, and their marshwallow, Lark. In fact, Lark gets a special mention — what a lovely, gentle giant.

Meanwhile, Mirri and Deidre engaged in a delicate dance of deception with Agate, feigning interest in this ‘new god’ that Agate kept mentioning; that is, apparently, going to be welcomed. For a change, it didn't take me an eternity to figure out the obvious: Agate was talking about Vestan. I didn't hear all of the details, but what I heard – talk of blessed darkness?! – concerned me greatly.

As we attempted to help with the wagon's stuck wheel, my clumsy efforts resulted in an embarrassing face-plant. Sometimes, I fear my usefulness to this group is limited, especially in matters requiring even small amounts of finesse and shrewdness.

Oh, well, they're stuck with me for now, I suppose.

I can hardly even believe I just wrote that. I came into the Feywild feeling fine; comfortable with myself. I believe this place has gone beyond humbling me …
