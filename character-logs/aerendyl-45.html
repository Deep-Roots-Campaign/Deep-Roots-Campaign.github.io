[Session 45] Day 4 of Firecrest, T.A. 515? | Day 13 in the Feywild
Our planned ambush of the cultists in the Underfey was anything but straightforward. Our advantage – from being in the shadows – was, however, short-lived. The others on the other side of the chamber alerted the cultists somehow, and they were about to be found out.

Corvaux apparently didn’t want that to happen, so she suddenly charged, tackling the orc cultist nearest to our group before Mirri and Deidre could even don their disguises. It was a reckless move, one I couldn't understand, that thrust us into chaos. I adore Corvaux, I do, but I’ll admit to not being a fan of recklessness, let alone just now — even if it’s saved my hide more than once until now. The stakes are just so incredibly high right now …

And just as things couldn't seem to get any worse, a chilling voice echoed from the darkness across the lake. A priestess, her words dripping with arrogance and malice, piqued a sense of paranoia in me I haven’t ever felt before. To make matters worse: she’d extinguished the fire burning there, rendering it into a pitch black corner.

I could only press on, hoping there wasn’t a bridge in the darkness somewhere behind me, leading me to join Corvaux on the precarious bridge, crowding the orc. The bridge creaked below us; it could crumble at any minute.

And then, of course, another, heavily armored figure revealed their voice near the priestess, extinguishing the fire the moment Mirri relit it. Hardly any later, a searing blast of fire cut through the chamber, leaving me both grateful for being just out of range and horrified for Grout's safety; he’d stayed behind at my behest, but he was cut off from me by the fire. Fuck.

Once the orc was downed, Merisande and I pressed forward, crossing the rickety bridge. Battling these followers of Vestan, I felt a strange mixture of anger and reluctance — even though their cult had stolen my father from me, the act of taking their lives sat heavy on my heart. Granted, some we would keep alive to be interrogated, but knowing death ultimately awaited them all the same … I don’t know.

I understand the necessity, but I have never killed or let another kill with such cold calculation before.

I didn’t have much of an opportunity to think of it at the time: Mirri stumbled from the other bridge leading towards the priestess and the knight, her eyes vacant, replaced with a chilling emptiness. She had only Rory’s eyes to see through now, so I decided to do what I should have likely done long ago, and charged forwards, straight toward the two. There, I also caught a glimpse of Luck fumbling to keep his head up. I’m still not sure what happened there, but all the same we managed to focus our efforts on the last of our immediate adversaries.

While Luck dealt the final blow, the flames from the earlier fireball crept towards us, engulfing the bridge. Mirri, once she’d regained her sight with Deidre’s help, extinguished the flame approaching us, fortunately. She is, if nothing else, an extremely effective problem-solver …

Interestingly, the dagger that caused her blindness before, though, just about melted into some ichorous fluid, leaving only a few shards behind. I wonder if the same might happen if we try shattering the obsidian dagger I took from the cultists in Nordthorn so many years ago. Idle curiosity doesn’t seem valid enough a reason to try ruining a weapon that might come in handy at some point or other, though, so … let’s not.

As we searched the fallen cultists, their tattoos, weapons, and a stubbornly unremovable ring hinted at darker forces at play. Deidre sensed a history of death in this place, a palpable aura that even she, with her connection to the spectral realm, couldn't fully grasp.

Luck performed a solemn funeral rite for the dead, a small act of respect – to the fallen, the Raven Queen, or both? – amid the grim reality of our actions. The weight of the orc's red robes, given to me as a disguise going forwards, felt – and still feel – like a heavy cloak of shame, and not just because they’re in dire need of patching up.

Deidre's curiosity led her to a massive archway by the waterfall, its keyhole gaping like a maw. Despite our warnings, she inserted her hand, becoming unresponsive, her body turning cold and rigid.

I was unnerved; the sight reminded me of my harrowing encounter with Eristalon. Turns out that that is precisely what it was, too. Granted, I could only overhear her conversation with Mirri once she’d come to and dashed off, but the gist of it was that he demanded a party be sent to them (us, probably), and she refused, instead – somehow – persuading him to agree to us delivering him Vestan, or his soul, I suppose.

I’m not sure how I feel about that, but I suppose if anyone’s soul was to go to that menace of an entity, it had better be someone’s who is at least just as cruel.

Still, I worry now: has Eristalon still taken something from Deidre, a precious memory or a fragment of her essence, that she was hiding from us? Eristalon would not have let me go had I not given something up that was a part of my very essence, so could he really have let her go with a mere promise to deliver Vestan in the future?
