Flamecrest, 515 of 3A.
Day 13 in the Feywilds

The little balls of light were Will-o-wisps; they were fast and nearly impossible to hit. But we managed in the end. Luck and Corvaux took watch on each end of this crystal cavern. While we rested and those who were wounded recuperated… I wandered back into the main cavern.

The water was murky, but still enough for me to catch a glimpse of my reflection. The swirling aurora was still there in my eyes and I was starting to wonder if the effect was permanent. The thought of it left me… conflicted. I focused on the pattern, on my appearance as a whole. I have magic now that can change it, far easier than any costume or cosmetic I’ve used before, and when I opened my eyes again, they were back to normal. Dark hazel brown. So I do have some control over it!

What I did next was purely selfish. Self-indulgent. Something I had never dared attempt before… I changed my eyes again. As soft and green as the fields of Orllanar. To see my face staring back at me with their eyes… It was surreal. It brought up a thousand “what-ifs”, things I had wondered all my life. Would it have been easier if I looked like this instead of…

Back to brown. The green doesn’t suit me. I wish it did.

We decided that our time here would be longer than originally expected. There was the muddy cavern we had only barely investigated and so that was where I headed first while Aeren and Luck returned to fetch Grout and Ralph. There was a massive curtain of ivy and small green motes dancing in the air. It brought to mind the shrine to Mielikki in the house above. Leaving Rorschach behind, Smoke, Deidre, and I slipped quietly into the tunnel, following it until it opened into another cavern… a shrine of some kind? Smoke excitedly said that this must be a temple to Mielikki and her sisters. But my greatest surprise was that there was a shrine to the Librarian here.

The moment Smoke and Deidre stepped away, I tried to invoke her. Do you pray to an Archfey like you do the gods? I haven’t done that since I was a small girl, but I had to try. I asked for help defeating him. And, if I must be specific… I asked about the Eldoumiir Line. It has been on my mind since before we arrived here in the Feywild—the Archive said he was a descendant of that line. The root of all of this—literally & figuratively— may lie with them. Who were they? What happened to them? Are they still around? Will I find Xyrra’s name among them or was she a lie, too?

It wasn’t until Aeren returned with Luck that we understood exactly where we were; this is the Serenity of the Sisters!

What joy I felt at knowing this was soon snatched away as Aeren began to translate the Druidic text on the ceiling mural. The Sun Devourer. The eclipse, the doorway, the Abyss, the Shadowfell. Fuck. Fuck! So this is how he means to do it? His ascent delivered on a silver spoon with this doorway?

And yet… it might also be a chance I have to take. What if that opening is my chance to free Hallow? The very thought of it is madness, that I might walk in his footsteps in any way, but it’s something to keep in mind. Maybe the Librarian can help me with that, too. I don’t know, nor do I have enough faith in the archfey to risk it. What I do know is that I at least owe it to Hallow to tell her what we’ve learned as soon as possible. The others are exploring the temple. Aeren and Deidre are talking—didn’t they pray at the shrine in the manor? Maybe they’re trying again. Corvaux’s gone to peer into that deep crevice at the final pool. I’ve decided to sit by the Librarian’s shrine and try to reach Hallow. This kind of information cannot wait.

The Lochathi was waiting. It was stupid and reckless and I should not have done it. Stop it. Stop it, let me go. Let me—

I have to get you home. I promised. I tried, Mama…. I’m sorry. I’m sorry. I tried. But I couldn’t… It wasn’t— I’m bleeding. My blood and his on my hands get it off get it off get it off— Cal, I’ll wait for you at the pier. I waited and waited and waited and you never came—
