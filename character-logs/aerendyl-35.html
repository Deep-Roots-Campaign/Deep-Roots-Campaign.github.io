Aerendyl 35

Day 1 of Flamecrest, year 515 of T.A.?
Amidst the chaos of our escape, Mirri's voice echoed in my mind, urging me to give the key to ‘him’ – I assumed she meant the dragon, but, in hindsight, she might have meant the stranger I called out to, but didn't respond ... But before I could even really act, the stranger's words cut through the air, a barrage of insults that struck at the very core of the dragon's pride. To my astonishment, the beast fled, its vines loosening their grip on Corvaux.

As I reached the roof, I beheld a sight that chilled me to the bone. Corvaux, transformed into a gaunt, spectral banshee if some sort, knelt beside Deidre's motionless form. I channeled my healing magic into her, pulling her back from the brink of death – albeit just barely. Back down I ran then, prompted by the dragon's escape to flee this place with everyone, upon which Corvaux's bag of holding landed at my feet, a puzzling omen …

The dragon's fury knew no bounds, though, and its return proved it. It found its way to where I wound up just outside, distracted by a booted foot poking out of the rubble (the stranger, as it later turned out). The dragon lashed out at me with its vine-like tail, striking with devastating force. I only just had the right thought to summon a shield to my aid, and reflect some of the damage done. The stranger's firebolt seared the beast's exterior, and Corvaux, in a display of reckless bravery, leapt onto the dragon's back, grappling for control.

Desperation fuelled my actions. I hurled the cold iron key into the dragon's gaping maw, praying it would be enough to halt its rampage. But the key's power seemed to do little, and the dragon's eyes, now multiplied by the shifting vines, continued their relentless search.

But was it really useless? Before long, the creature seemed … nauseous; unstable — could it have poisoned it?

As the stranger and Corvaux fought on, I could only watch in helpless frustration. My spells seemed to bounce off the dragon's hide, serving only to draw its ire. Deidre's quick thinking, a vial of oil thrown into the beast's face, combined with another firebolt, finally brought the creature to its knees. In a blaze of searing heat, the dragon crumbled to ashes right before me, its snapping jaws faltering instantly.

Relief warred with shame as I surveyed the aftermath. My contributions felt paltry, my inadequacy laid bare. I pocketed a dragon tooth and the cold iron key, small tokens of our harrowing victory and tools for later use, even as the stranger's disguise melted away to reveal Mirri's familiar face. Well, stranger … now out of the heat of battle, I could put two and two together — that was Vestan's face. I wonder about the toll that must have taken on her; to wear the visage of the begetter of her agony ...

As we tended to our wounds and that question fell to the back of my mind, a group of masked rangers approached, led by a figure who introduced herself as Dasha. Her earthy complexion and vaguely Terran accent hinted at a shared heritage, a fleeting solace in this alien land — a touch of solace, at least. What happened in that vault has sullied what comfort I took in the thought of the Earth Plane to large enough a degree to make that sense of recognition feel equal parts comforting and alienating.

Deidre's unease seemingly anchored to the ranger with the wolf mask piqued my curiosity, so as Mirri and Corvaux conversed with the rangers, I joined her. As we sat beneath a tree, I probed gently, sensing a deeper fear lurking beneath her preference for felines. But exhaustion and the weight of the day's events stayed my tongue. Instead, I lost myself in introspection, questioning my choices, my diminished longing for home, and our chances against the looming threat of Vestan.

The rangers proved invaluable allies, providing a map and warnings of the dangers ahead. Alexandra, a fairy resistant to charm, eagerly joined our ranks, her griffin companion Roe a welcome addition. As a parting gift, we were given two paper birds, allowing us to keep in touch with them as needed.

While we bade farewell to our new allies (though, in the face of Deidre's suspicion towards them, I use that word cautiously), I couldn't help but dwell on the unsent paper bird tucked away in my bag — Mirri's final words, entrusted to me should the worst come to pass. If nothing else, it served as a harsh reminder of the danger we have yet to face, even as we set out to track Luck and Ralph's path into the forest before all else.

Through the cracked stone and verdant foliage a ways off, their trail was clear. I have no doubt now that we'll find our two wayward companions soon — and then, I think I should like some proper rest. It's been a short day, but, Gods, am I tired, and in need of some time to think.
