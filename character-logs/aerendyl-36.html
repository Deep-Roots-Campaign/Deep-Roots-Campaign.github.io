Aerendyl 36

Day 1 and 2 of Flamecrest, T.A. 515?
Having found Luck and Ralph, we made camp to rest and discuss the events in the vault. I confided in the group about sacrificing my attachment to my mother, which seemed to deeply affected Mirri. She cupped my face, our eyes meeting, and vowed to reunite me with my mother.

Her dedication moved me to the brink of tears, or maybe it was her sympathy that did it. Either way, I wonder just what it'll take for her to fulfil that promise. Given where we are now, the challenges we yet face and the distance between us and my home.

Or, former home? I struggle to come up with the right words to describe my sentiments on that place now. It is home, surely, yet at the same time I no longer yearn for it quite so strongly. I think my mother's presence there made me long to go back there the way I did.

I love her still. That, I know. I did not relinquish my love for her, or even for the place she raised me in. But so many of the memories we shared, those that both elated and tortured me? Gone, a vacuum void left in their stead.

But I must go back – to ensure she is safe; to ensure Vestan's lackeys didn't find her after Mirri and Runa freed my father. He and his undoubtedly have a vengeful streak that I'm right to worry about.

Either way, my gratitude towards everyone for their roles in protecting me and the staff during the battle remains unchanged. Having faced what we did, then, I prepared Grout for future dangers, intensifying his training to flee on command.

Before it was time to head to bed for the first several hours, I attuned to the staff. I heard the star's song again. This time, I found myself fully able to appreciate the Celestial verse. Arguably more impressive was how, somehow, without exerting any explicit effort, I felt as though my ties to nature were strengthened. Not only that: but casting Light upon the staff's geode unleashed a mesmerizing star chart, ancient yet evolving to reflect the current sky. I marvelled at the staff's otherworldly aura, strongly feeling that I had only begun to unravel its mysteries.

I also got word out to Wisp about the star; at least that's one less thing for her to worry about.

Deidre and I unearthed some interesting topics during our shared watch after I slept for a few hours. She revealed her lack of a fate line on her hand. Odd, I think, given the circumfluence of all these others bewildering circumstances around her existence. We mused about what it might mean; a lack of a predestined fate, or her fate depending on someone else's — on this group's, perhaps?

She also confessed to feeling a lingering compulsion to return to the mansion, fearing retribution for her absence. Did whatever Mirri and she got up to below the mansion not fully dissolve her ties to that place, then?

She spoke of someone following her, too — of thinking that much, at least, and despite not having seen anyone myself, her dismissal of it as it being ‘in her mind’ doesn't much convince me. Instead of pushing the matter, I introduced her to the Earth Plane custom of the ‘pinky promise,’ making her swear to alert us if she ever sensed someone pursuing her.

After our watch, sleep ushered me into a harrowing dreamscape where I encountered … eternity. An eternity in agony, in fact, preceded by an ocean of time spent in the cold, dark nothingness of space, watching star after star being snuffed out — before I myself was, in a sense. It was then that I found myself well and truly trapped, questioning my sanity and reality with to the point where it became brain-numbing.

Numb would have been a welcome feeling, in fact. True numbness, that is, but I wasn't granted even a taste of it before I suddenly materialised at the bottom of a ravine, walled in by walls of rock set with geodes casting out refractive light, beckoned by the moon hanging overhead. It reminded me of the Whistling Valleys once the wind started blowing past me ...

Suddenly, then, a great red gate appeared before me, but once I'd blinked, it was gone. In its stead stood a tall, ethereal looking stranger, quite fey-like in appearance, their hair white as snow, eyes smattered with constellations unseen, and skin decorated with gold-lined, purple cloud formations.

Oh, I'll admit to it: I was frightened — again. I knew not what to make of the figure, of the particular lilt in their voice, their levitated form, the pet names they bestowed upon me. It felt like a slight to my ego, in all honesty, but the more I learned about them, the more I felt that it was not meant that way … at least, I can hope as much.

Their name is Toraash, and they stated they would be my best friend going forward. Now, as eager as I am to believe that, truly, I … must confess to feeling some apprehension. Maybe Mirri's warnings got to me, finally; maybe the rude awakenings I've been subjected to the last few days have dispelled my usual gullibility. Whatever is the case: their mere promise of wanting to be a teacher to me does not yet instil me with the confidence I require to trust them fully.

Not after my first lesson ( in empathy, I suppose ) was my journey towards them; that eternity I mentioned; that eternity of agony. I wondered for a moment what I had done to deserve such a harsh lesson, but perhaps it was never a matter of ‘deserving’, but rather a matter of necessity. Maybe this was what it took for Toraash to speak to me in plainer terms, compared to the song. They did mention it would be easier to talk henceforth, so I’ll choose gullibility and believe in that, at least.

I despise my lack of utter trust, though. I miss my naivety, because it felt wrong to avoid their questions, to answer so vaguely, even though it felt necessary to protect my friends and myself from some hidden danger, perhaps. Nevertheless, some part of me did also believe Toraash was curious merely for having been imprisoned for centuries – since sometime in the Second Age, apparently!

I hope they took no offence to it all, in any event. They seemed understanding at face value, at least; or patient, at least, in their search to learn more of my world (worlds?). At the same time, I know now they are aware of listening to conversations taking place around the staff, and thus I wonder if I should exercise caution; warn the others of this — even if, the more I think of it, I imagine Toraash knowing of the current events could also be an immense boon to our cause …

Upon waking, the dream's vivid details and the iridescent shimmer of my fate lines lingered, hinting at the profound changes on the horizon. Once the shock had worn off, and I felt anchored in my body once more, I rushed to show Deidre and tell her of the sequence in my sleep that preceded the change in my fate lines. Granted, it could just be the result of digging my nails into my palms, but the placement … Well, I just find ‘coincidence’ too easy an explanation for a great deal of things, I suppose.

Mirri's ichor-streaked face greeted me out of nowhere mid-conversation, a side effect of communing with her patron. She urged me to postpone my questions until after breakfast, but my appetite had vanished, replaced by an overwhelming yearning to leave this place that had so ruthlessly stripped away my trust and my cherished connection to home.

I longed for the simplicity of Tornwing Haven, to serve and protect that small community in harmony with nature. Yet, I knew there was no turning back from the dark truths I had uncovered. I had to press forward, grateful for my companions who had proven their unwavering support, for Grout's loyal presence, and for the cautious hope I placed in Toraash's benevolence. May my sacrifice not have been vain, and may Toraash prove to be the ally I so desperately need them to be.
